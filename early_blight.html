Brief Description:

Early blight, caused by the fungus Alternaria solani, affects tomato plants, especially when they are stressed or older. It is most identifiable by the formation of dark, circular spots on the lower leaves. These spots have a "target-like" appearance due to concentric rings. The disease spreads upwards, causing leaves to yellow, wither, and fall off. It can also cause sunken, dark lesions on stems and fruit, typically near the stem end.

Prevention and Control:

Rotate crops, use drip irrigation to avoid wetting leaves, and prune lower leaves to prevent spores from splashing up from the soil. Good air circulation is key. Remove and destroy all infected plant debris at the end of the season. Fungicides containing chlorothalonil or copper can be applied as a preventive measure, especially in warm, humid conditions.

Effective Treatments:

Fungicides with active ingredients like chlorothalonil or copper are effective. They create a protective barrier on the plant's surface to prevent the fungal spores from germinating and infecting the plant.
