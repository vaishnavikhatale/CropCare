Potato: Late Blight

Brief Description:

Late blight, caused by the water mold Phytophthora infestans, is one of the most devastating diseases for tomato plants. It begins with irregular, water-soaked spots on leaves that quickly turn brown and black. A key diagnostic sign is the **white, fuzzy growth** that appears on the underside of the leaves in cool, moist conditions. The disease progresses rapidly, causing entire plants to collapse and fruit to rot.

Prevention and Control:

This disease can be difficult to manage once it takes hold. Use resistant tomato varieties and avoid planting near potatoes, as they are a common host. Ensure proper plant spacing and stake plants to improve air circulation. Avoid overhead watering. If an outbreak occurs, remove and destroy infected plants immediately to prevent the spread of spores.

Effective Treatments:

Fungicides with active ingredients like Mancozeb or copper are effective. They work best as a preventive measure, applied at the first sign of disease and repeated regularly during cool, wet weather. Prompt and thorough application is crucial.
