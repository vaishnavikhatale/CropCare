Peas Plant

Scientific NamePisum sativum
FamilyFabaceae
TypeLeguminous vegetable crop

Basic Information

Peas are among the oldest cultivated crops in the world. They are cool-season vegetables, best grown in winter or early spring. The plant is a climber, supported by tendrils. Peas grow in pods, which contain several round seeds. They are rich in protein, vitamins, and dietary fiber. Peas are eaten fresh, dried, or processed. They help improve soil fertility by fixing nitrogen. The plant grows best in well-drained loamy soil. Peas require regular irrigation but not waterlogging. Flowers are usually white or purple. Peas are used in soups, curries, and salads. They are important for vegetarian diets due to high protein. Harvesting is done when pods are tender. Storage requires low temperatures to maintain freshness. Pea plants are sensitive to frost at flowering stage. Crop rotation prevents soil diseases. Peas are often intercropped with cereals. They provide food and income for farmers. Green pea farming is popular worldwide. The crop cycle is short, usually 3â€“4 months.

How to Solve Peas Plant Issues

Use healthy certified seeds for sowing. Practice proper crop rotation. Treat seeds with fungicides before sowing. Avoid waterlogging to prevent root rot. Spray sulfur for powdery mildew. Grow resistant pea varieties. Destroy and remove diseased plants. Apply balanced fertilizers and compost. Use organic sprays like neem oil for pest control. Provide trellis support for healthy growth.
