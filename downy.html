Brief Description:

Downy mildew is a fungal disease that thrives in cool, wet conditions. It appears as yellow-green spots on the upper side of leaves, which later turn brown. On the underside, a characteristic white, fluffy growth of fungal spores is visible. Severe infections can lead to leaf drop, stunted growth, and a significant reduction in yield. The disease can also affect young stems and fruits, causing them to shrivel and rot.

Prevent This Plant Disease By following these steps:

Improve air circulation by pruning and proper plant spacing. Use resistant grape varieties. Apply preventative fungicides during periods of wet weather, especially when new growth is present. Remove and destroy infected leaves and plant debris to reduce the spread of spores. Avoid overhead watering to keep foliage dry.

Supplements:

Copper-based fungicides like Copper Oxychloride or Bordeaux mixture are effective against downy mildew. They create a protective barrier on the plant surface that prevents fungal spores from germinating.
