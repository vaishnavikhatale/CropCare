Brief Description:

Powdery mildew is a fungal disease that appears as white, powdery spots on leaves, stems, and grapes. It thrives in dry, humid conditions and can affect new and old growth. Infected leaves may turn yellow and curl, while fruit can become stunted, cracked, or discolored, which severely impacts the yield and quality. The disease can spread quickly, covering entire leaves in a fine, white powder.

Prevent This Plant Disease By following these steps:

Ensure good air circulation by proper pruning and spacing. Use resistant grape varieties if available. Apply horticultural oils or sulfur-based fungicides as a preventative measure. Remove and destroy infected plant parts to prevent further spread. Avoid excess nitrogen fertilization, which can promote new, susceptible growth.

Supplements:

Sulfur-based fungicides are very effective in controlling powdery mildew. They work by preventing the germination of fungal spores and are a common choice for organic growers.
