Brief Description:

Tomato Leaf Curl Virus is a viral disease spread by whiteflies. It causes young leaves to curl upwards, become brittle, and turn yellow between the veins. Plants infected early in their growth are severely stunted with a bushy appearance and shortened shoots. Flower and fruit production is drastically reduced or stopped entirely. There is no cure, so management focuses on prevention.

Prevention and Control:

The best way to manage this virus is to control the whitefly vector. Use virus-free seedlings and resistant varieties. Use yellow sticky traps to monitor for whiteflies. If whiteflies are present, use insecticidal soap or neem oil to control their population. Promptly remove and destroy any infected plants to prevent further spread.

Effective Treatments:

Since this is a virus, there is no chemical treatment to cure the plant. However, controlling the whitefly population is critical. Insecticidal soaps and neem oil are effective at reducing the whitefly numbers, which in turn reduces the transmission of the virus.
