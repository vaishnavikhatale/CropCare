Brief Description:

Anthracnose is a fungal disease that causes dark, sunken lesions on soyabean leaves, stems, and berries. On leaves, it creates small, circular, or irregular spots that are often dark brown or black. On fruit, it's known as "bird's-eye rot," causing distinctive, circular, sunken spots with a dark border. The fungus thrives in warm, rainy weather and can cause significant damage to the plant , leading to reduced yield and quality.

Prevent This Plant Disease By following these steps:

Prune out and destroy infected canes and plant debris during the dormant season. Apply a dormant spray of lime sulfur to reduce overwintering inoculum. Ensure good air circulation and avoid overhead irrigation. Use protectant fungicides like Mancozeb or Copper-based products, especially during periods of wet weather when new growth is present.

Supplements:

Mancozeb is a broad-spectrum, protectant fungicide effective against anthracnose. It works by preventing the fungus from establishing itself on the plant tissue.
